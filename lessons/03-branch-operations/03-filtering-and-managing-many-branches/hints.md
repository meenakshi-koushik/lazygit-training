## Hint 1

You need to work in the **Branches** panel. There are a lot of branches in this repo -- scrolling through all of them to find the `chore/` branches is slow. Lazygit has a way to filter the list. Look for a search/filter keybinding.

## Hint 2

In the Branches panel, press `/` to open the filter bar. Type `chore` to narrow the branch list to only branches containing "chore" in their name. You should see three branches: `chore/update-deps`, `chore/ci-pipeline`, and `chore/cleanup-logs`.

## Hint 3

With the filter active and a `chore/` branch highlighted, press `d` to delete it. Lazygit will ask for confirmation -- press `<enter>` to confirm. Repeat for each of the three chore branches. When you are done, press `<esc>` to clear the filter and confirm the chore branches are gone. Make sure you are on `main` before finishing.
